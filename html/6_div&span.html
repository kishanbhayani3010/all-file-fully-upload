Chhota Bheem and Kalia’s Lesson

In the peaceful village of Dholakpur, the sun rose bright and golden. Children were already playing near the river, and Chhota Bheem was enjoying a big bowl of laddoos given by Tun Tun Mausi. Everything seemed normal, but Kalia, as usual, was feeling jealous. Kalia watched Bheem from a distance and muttered, “Everyone only praises Bheem. Today, I will prove that I am better than him.” He came up with a plan to impress the king and make Bheem look foolish.

That afternoon, the king announced that a strong storm was expected, and the old bridge connecting the village to the farms had become weak. If the bridge broke, villagers would not be able to bring food supplies. The king asked for volunteers to help fix it. Before Bheem could step forward, Kalia loudly said, “Your Majesty, I will repair the bridge all by myself. I don’t need Bheem’s help!” The villagers were surprised, but the king agreed and wished him luck. Kalia went to the bridge with Dholu and Bholu. At first, he felt confident, but soon he realized the bridge was heavier and more damaged than he thought. The strong wind made it even harder to work. Suddenly, one wooden plank broke, and Kalia slipped, hanging dangerously over the river. “Help! Help!” Kalia shouted in fear. Hearing the noise, Chhota Bheem rushed to the spot. Without thinking twice, he grabbed a rope, jumped forward, and pulled Kalia up to safety. Dholu and Bholu were relieved. Bheem then said calmly, “Kalia, fixing the bridge needs teamwork. Let’s do it together.” Ashamed and tired, Kalia nodded. For the first time, he said, “I’m sorry, Bheem. I was wrong.” Together, Bheem, Kalia, and the villagers repaired the bridge before the storm arrived. When the king came to inspect it, he was very pleased. “Today, I learned something important,” the king said. “Strength is good, but helping others and working together is true greatness.” Kalia looked at Bheem and said softly, “Thank you for saving me.” Bheem smiled and replied, “We are all part of Dholakpur.” That evening, everyone celebrated. Even Kalia joined the villagers happily, realizing that friendship is stronger than jealousy. Nobita and the Day He Didn’t Give Up

One sunny morning in Tokyo, Nobita was rushing to school, already late as usual. He tripped on the road and fell flat. “Why am I always so unlucky?” he sighed. Doraemon floated beside him and said, “Nobita, today try something different—don’t give up so easily.” At school, the teacher announced a science project competition. The winner would get a special medal and praise from the whole school. Nobita felt nervous. He was not good at studies, and Gian and Suneo immediately started boasting about how they would win. During recess, Gian laughed and said, “Nobita, you should just watch us win.” Suneo added, “Yes, science is not for you.” Nobita felt sad and ran home crying. At home, Doraemon listened patiently. “Doraemon, I can never win anything,” Nobita said. Doraemon smiled and replied, “You don’t need gadgets every time. This time, try using your own ideas.”

Nobita thought hard and remembered how he loved watching plants grow on his balcony. He decided to make a simple project on how plants grow better with care and kindness. Every day, he watered the plants, talked to them, and observed them carefully. It was slow work, but Nobita didn’t quit. On the day of the competition, many students brought fancy models. Nobita felt scared again, but Doraemon whispered, “Just explain honestly.” When it was Nobita’s turn, he spoke about patience, care, and responsibility. He showed how his plant grew healthier because of regular attention. The class listened quietly. To everyone’s surprise, the teacher smiled and said, “This project is simple, but the effort is amazing.” Nobita won second prize. It wasn’t first place, but it was his biggest achievement yet. Gian and Suneo were shocked. Shizuka clapped happily and said, “Good job, Nobita!” That evening, Nobita looked at the medal and said, “Doraemon, I did it without any gadget.” Doraemon smiled proudly. “See? You were capable all along.” From that day on, Nobita still made mistakes—but he learned one important thing: Trying sincerely is more important than winning. 🌱✨
